What I've Found and Where!

This page is the odds and bobs I've found that appeal to me on some level. Whether I find them fun, interesting, useful, thought provoking or something else all together, some part of my crow brain found value in keeping these and now sharing them with you. I hope you enjoy. If not that's ok too. Different strokes for different folks and all that. And remember I'm just a person on the internet, please always do your own research!

Táin Bó Cuailnge

On my other page, Places To Go , you'll see one of the places I have listed is Rathcroghan. In the description, quoted from the Rathcroghan Visitor Page, you'll see mention of the Táin Bó Cuailnge, or Cattle Raid of Cooley referenced. While I heard of Rathcroghan last year, I've only started learning more about it, the surrounding area, and the Táin more recently and mostly through the youtube page, blog and Irish Pagan School of Lora O'Brien. Linked below is a reading of the Táin Bó Cuailnge, with some commentary. It is an ongoing work, and labour of love for Irish author and Irish Pagan Priest Lora O'Brien, meaning it may not be complete by the time you see this page. If you are curious to read ahead the sources are shared. I hope you enjoy, and if you do please check out the other stuff on Lora's pages, and you know, the regular youtube stuff of liking, subscribing, and sharing.

You can find more of Lora O'Brien on Youtube, Facebook, Instagram their blog, or The Irish Pagan School.

Future Learn

One of the more positive side effects of the socially-distant, stay-home, culture that has developed in response to the Covid crisis is the increased ability to access things online, and the highlighting of things that were already there. Many of these at low costs or free. One of the ones I found was Future Learn. Because I'm currently in a new area with a unique culture and language that differs from what I've always known, I had seeked out places to learn more and was recommended Irish 101: An Introduction to Irish Langugage and Culture course. This course is free from Dublin City University through Future Learn.

Don't forget to check out what else is available through Future learn as it changes frequently.

Books- Emma Dean

One of my current favorite authors is Emma Dean. Not only for the wonderful, indepth characters and worlds she has created but for the inspiration she is as a person. Her writing genre of choice is primarily fantasy romance (witches, shifters and demons, oh my), and science fantasy fiction romance. Though I have not read her Draga series (intrigue with intriguing aliens), yet, I have read most of her other works in the other universe she has created. In her works, which are 18+, she touches on many sensitive and relavent topics that divide and bring us together through the lens of her works. These are including but not limited to mental health, trauma, perserverance, constructed divisions people make between themselves, and what makes family family. And of course love, I mean, they are romance, though it's not just romantic love that's explored.

Want to know more? Check her out on facebook, and her work on goodreads, and amazon.co.uk, amazon.ca. or amazon.com .

Sport Ireland

While searching for a particular trail for this post I came on section of a great site aimed towards getting people in Ireland outdoors more. This includes walking, hiking, running and cycling trail information, as well as info for adventure and some water related sports. So instead of a particular trail I found many. And while I’m going to aim for the top, the highest point in Ireland, Carrauntoohil, in Co Kerry, eventually, I have no problem admitting I am no where near ready for that hike yet. So to work up to that goal I will likely be using the fantastic resources provided by Sport Ireland Outdoors.
