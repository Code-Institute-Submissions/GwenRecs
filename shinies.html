Táin Bó Cuailnge

On the next page, Places To Go , you'll see one of the places I have listed is Rathcroghan. In the description, quoted from the Rathcroghan visitor page , you'll see the Táin Bó Cuailnge, or Cattle Raid of Cooley (sp?) referenced. While I heard of Rathcroghan last year, I've only started learning more about it, the surrounding area and the Táin more recently and mostly through the youtube page, blog and Irish Pagan School of Lora O'Brien. Linked below is a reading of the Táin Bó Cuailnge, with some commentary. It is an ongoing work, and labour of love for , meaning it may not be complete by the time you see this page. If you are curious to read ahead the sources are shared. I hope you enjoy, and if you do please check out the other stuff on Lora's pages, and you know, the regular youtube stuff of liking and sharing.

Future Learn

One of the more positive side effects of the socially-distant, stay-home, culture that has developed in response to the Covid crisis is the increased ability to access things online, and the highlighting of things that were already there. Many of these at low costs or free. One of the ones I found was Future Learn. Because I'm currently in a new area with a unique culture and language that differs from what I've always known, I had seeked out places to learn more and was recommended Irish 101: An Introduction to Irish Langugage and Culture course. This course is free from Dublin City University through Future Learn. https://www.futurelearn.com/courses/irish-language. Don't forget to check out what else is on offer there, it changes frequently.
